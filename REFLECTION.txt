REFLECTION

What went wrong: My first attempts trusted assumptions that broke in practice.
I targeted Python 3.14 (no ML wheels), let RandomForest train with unbounded
depth so long background runs got killed, rendered the map with Plotly's d3-geo
engine (a polygon winding-order bug flooded the whole frame), used a seaborn
colorscale Plotly rejects (crashing the Analytics tab), and made rapid GDELT
calls until the API rate-limited the news fetch.

How I adjusted: I stopped assuming and verified each component against the
running system. I pinned Python 3.12, capped and split training into shorter
stages, switched the map to the maplibre renderer, used only valid colorscales,
and fetched news in throttled, incremental chunks.

Why the final version is better: it is reproducible, fails gracefully, is
honestly evaluated (news lift came out at about -2%, so I kept it optional),
and -- unlike the first attempt -- actually runs end to end and is deployed and
verified online.
